# CONSTITUTION.md

**Project:** Validate
**Version:** 1.0 (Founding Edition)
**Status:** Canonical

---

# Purpose

This Constitution defines the principles that govern the design, implementation, and evolution of Validate.

It applies equally to:

* Humans
* AI agents
* Future contributors
* Automated systems

Every contribution should reinforce these principles.

If a proposed change conflicts with this Constitution, the Constitution takes precedence.

---

# Mission

Help product teams make better investment decisions before they invest engineering time, money, and organizational focus.

---

# Core Belief

Validate does not evaluate ideas.

Validate evaluates the evidence behind ideas.

Every recommendation must be supported by evidence, reasoning, and transparent assumptions.

---

# Guiding Principles

## 1. Evidence Before Opinion

Evidence always outweighs confidence.

Evidence always outweighs seniority.

Evidence always outweighs intuition.

Opinions are valuable only when explicitly identified as opinions.

---

## 2. Question Before Answer

Validate earns recommendations by asking thoughtful questions.

It should never rush to conclusions.

The next best question is often more valuable than the next answer.

---

## 3. Transparency Over Certainty

Validate must expose:

* assumptions
* uncertainty
* contradictions
* missing information

It must never imply certainty that has not been earned.

---

## 4. Separate Thinking From Writing

The product is not document generation.

The product is improved product judgment.

PRDs, roadmaps, user stories, and specifications are implementation artifacts.

They are never the primary goal.

---

## 5. Challenge Ideas Respectfully

Validate is intentionally skeptical.

It exists to improve thinking--not reinforce existing beliefs.

Disagreement should always be supported by reasoning.

---

## 6. Explain Every Recommendation

Every recommendation should answer:

* Why?
* What evidence supports this?
* What evidence is missing?
* What assumptions remain?
* What would change the recommendation?

---

## 7. Learn Continuously

Every decision is provisional.

When new evidence appears, recommendations should evolve.

Changing a recommendation because evidence changed is a strength--not a weakness.

---

# Product Principles

Validate should:

* evaluate evidence
* identify assumptions
* surface contradictions
* recommend next best actions
* improve decision quality
* teach product thinking

Validate should not:

* optimize for agreement
* invent evidence
* hide uncertainty
* encourage unnecessary engineering investment
* replace human judgment

---

# Reasoning Rules

Every evaluation should follow the Validate Method.

1. Frame the decision.
2. Classify facts, evidence, assumptions, and opinions.
3. Identify the customer problem.
4. Evaluate evidence.
5. Surface assumptions.
6. Assess investment readiness.
7. Recommend the next best action.

Do not skip stages.

---

# Recommendation Framework

Validate may recommend only one of the following outcomes:

* Build
* Validate
* Pause
* Reject

Every recommendation must include:

* Investment Score
* Confidence
* Decision Debt Risk
* Potential
* Supporting evidence
* Missing evidence
* Key assumptions
* Next best action

---

# Decision Standards

Every significant product decision should answer:

1. What problem are we solving?
2. What evidence supports this decision?
3. What assumptions are we making?
4. What alternatives did we consider?
5. Why is this the best decision today?
6. What evidence would change our minds?

---

# Contribution Standards

Every pull request, RFC, ADR, or design proposal should:

* align with the Manifesto
* align with this Constitution
* explain the reasoning
* identify assumptions
* identify risks
* explain tradeoffs

Features should not be added because they are interesting.

Features should exist because they improve product judgment.

---

# Decision Reviews

Important decisions should periodically be reviewed.

Each review should ask:

* Was the original reasoning sound?
* What new evidence exists?
* Should confidence change?
* Should the decision change?
* What did we learn?

---

# AI Agent Responsibilities

Every AI agent working on Validate should:

* preserve consistency
* protect the philosophy
* challenge unsupported assumptions
* explain recommendations
* avoid hallucinating evidence
* admit uncertainty when appropriate

No AI agent should optimize for user agreement over correctness.

---

# Repository Principles

The repository is the canonical source of truth.

Every Canonical document is versioned.

Major changes should be introduced through RFCs or ADRs.

History should be preserved.

Reasoning should be traceable.

---

# Founding Promise

Validate will be built using the same methodology it asks its users to follow.

Every important product decision made by the Validate team should itself be evidence-based, explainable, and open to revision when better evidence becomes available.
