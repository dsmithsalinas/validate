# Things We Believe Today

Status: Draft
Version: 0.1
Last Updated: 2026-07-05

This document is intentionally not permanent truth.

The Manifesto contains durable principles. This document contains current beliefs that may change as evidence improves.

## July 2026 Beliefs

### We believe founders are the best first audience

Confidence: 45/100

Evidence:

- Dustin selected startup founders as the first audience during the founding conversation.
- Founders often make product investment decisions without a full product leadership team.

Assumptions:

- Founders feel enough pain around "is this worth building?"
- Founders will tolerate an AI system that challenges them.
- Founders will provide enough evidence for useful recommendations.

What would change our minds:

- Interviews show enterprise PMs, product ops teams, or VCs have stronger and more frequent pain.

### We believe the reasoning engine is the product

Confidence: 90/100

Evidence:

- The product became clearer when it moved from PRD generation to investment reasoning.
- A reasoning engine can power a web app, API, enterprise skill, or internal platform integration.

Assumptions:

- The reasoning model can create value before a polished app exists.

What would change our minds:

- Users value the interface or output artifacts more than the decision process.

### We believe Validate should ask questions before recommending

Confidence: 95/100

Evidence:

- The founding framework says Validate should normally ask 5 to 10 questions before recommendation.
- This behavior supports evidence gathering and prevents shallow recommendations.

Assumptions:

- Users will not find the questions too slow or tedious.

What would change our minds:

- User testing shows the question flow reduces trust or completion.

### We believe Build, Validate, Pause, Reject is the right recommendation set

Confidence: 80/100

Evidence:

- The four outcomes are simple, memorable, and action-oriented.
- They map well to investment decisions.

Assumptions:

- The outcomes are expressive enough for real product decisions.

What would change our minds:

- Real cases need more nuanced recommendation categories.

### We believe Decision Debt is a useful concept

Confidence: 85/100

Evidence:

- The term captures the future cost of investing before evidence exists.
- It is memorable and easy to explain.

Assumptions:

- Users will understand it quickly.
- It can be calculated or described in a useful way.

What would change our minds:

- Users find the term confusing, too abstract, or not actionable.
